What is a virtual network?

A virtual network is a website, forum or a combination of both that hosts and promotes virtual series and operates as an actual television network does. You pitch a series to the network (you can find our guidelines here) and it's either greenlit or you'll receive timely feedback in order to shape your series up to be picked up by the network. Shows are renewed on a seasonal basis, etc.


What is a virtual series?

A virtual series is orginal or fan fiction written in as thought it were being filmed for television instead written like a novel (in other words, screenplay instead of prose).

  •   They can be written solo or as a team.
  •   On average, they have 6 to 24 episodes in a single season.
  •   Typical episode length for a half-hour series: 22-30 pages.
  •   Typical episode length for an hour long series: 50-60 pages.
  •   Webisodes typically have 6-12 episodes per sequence that range from 10-15 pages in length.
    •

    Like a real television series, your episodes are expected to "air" weekly. The network and readers aren't fond of long waits between episodes, so we require that you have a good portion of your episodes written prior to premiering your series.


    Do you do movies/mini-series?

    Absolutely! Our pitching/greenlighting guidelines vary a little between the different mediums, but we'll allow all of them. If you have an idea, just check out the information desk threads on our forums related to the content you want to pitch to us. If you have any questions, contact us via our contact page or send us a message on the forum!


    What if I already have a series and want to host it on EVPtv?

    If you've already written a series elsewhere and want to promote it through and air it on EVPtv, contact one of the network executives on the forums. We can discuss the options (will you need to pitch to us, will we bypass the process, etc.) and go from there.


    Can I work on a series that's already airing?

    Of course! But only if the showrunner wants you to. All showrunners have total creative control over their series and their crew, so they call all the shots. We won't force a showruller to do anything they don't want to do with their own crew.

    If there's a specific series you're interested in writing for, we suggest approaching the showrunner directly and (kindly!) asking if they need help. If you don't mind what project you're working on and just want the hands-on experience of working on a virtual series without the stress of running your own, check out the "looking for crew" section on the forums to see if there are any openings.
